Visualization of Employment and Illiteracy Rates amongst major religious groups in India

- Akash Sharma (2017327), Vidit Jain (2017370) and Akhil Jarodia (2017130)

Introduction

This is our interactive visualization for the analysis of inequality prevailing amongst major religious groups in India (Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism). Here, we try to visualise the employment rates and the illiteracy rates among these religious groups in particularly 4 dimensions - Rural Female , Rural Male , Urban Male and Urban Female. By adding these dimensions we get better and deeper insights into the data we are visualizing.

Visualization

Features of the Graph: You can hover over the graph to see the actual values of each bar (and its dimensions). You can also play with the scaling features to see selected zones of the graph like the illiteracy rates only. You can also dynamically drag/select the graph to see or hide certain portions of the graph to specific analysis.









About the Graph: This graph is a dual divergent graph which represents the illiteracy rates at right side of the zero line (y-axis) and labour force participation on the left side of the zero line. The illiteracy rate bar graphs starts from zero and extends to various values on the positive side of the x axis. Similarly, the labour force particpation rates bar graph extends from zero to the negative side of the x axis. [Here the negative signs of Labour Force Participation are just taken for the sake of representation on the graph and are not considered as negative values]. Each bar graph consists of 4 dimensions (each one of Urban-Male, Urban-Female, Rural-Male and Rural-Female).

Units of the graph: Both illiteracy rates and labour force participation rates are taken as per 1000 people per category. Eg) Number of illiterate rural female (Hindu) shown above is 378 out of 1000 rural female(Hindu).
